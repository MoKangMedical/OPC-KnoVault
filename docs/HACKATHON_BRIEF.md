# Monad Blitz Brief

Source: the public Notion package rendered through Playwright on 2026-05-12.

## Theme

Monad Blitz x The Mu 上海@V2: Agentic X.

Build a one-day, demonstrable Agentic X prototype on Monad testnet. The package explicitly says the goal is not a perfect market-ready app; it is to show a working core loop, explain the idea clearly, and explore Monad's high-throughput, low-latency EVM experience.

- First place: 600 USD.
- Second place: 500 USD.
- Third place: 400 USD.
- Top three: one Monad custom keycap keyboard each.

## Rules

- Team size: maximum 3 people.
- Projects must be new ideas specifically built for Monad Blitz.
- Existing projects and forks of existing codebases are not allowed, except standard libraries and boilerplate.
- Coding and asset creation must begin during the official Blitz period.
- Pre-event research, brainstorming, and planning are encouraged.
- The project should not be a direct clone unless it adds major innovation or a distinct Monad-specific feature.
- Submission materials must be public, for example a public GitHub repository.
- The project must be deployed and running on Monad for demo and review.
- The frontend must be publicly deployed and available for demo and review.
- Teams and submissions happen on MOJO; only the team lead can submit project details.
- Late submissions or code not committed during the competition window can affect eligibility.

## Submission Requirements

Submit through MOJO with:

- Logo.
- Preview image.
- Detailed introduction.
- Working preview link.
- GitHub link.

The package recommends clear, concise project copy. AI can be used for polishing, but not for long, low-signal filler.

## Judging

Judges decide based on:

- Whether the product satisfies the core requirements.
- Completion level.
- Whether the future business model is reasonable.

Participant voting also contributes during the scoring phase.

## Demo Guidance

- Each team has 5 minutes.
- The live Monad testnet demo should be the center of the presentation.
- Explain briefly how it was built, which Monad tools or features matter, and what technical challenge was solved.
- Highlight the strongest innovation instead of showing every feature.
- Slides are optional; simple architecture/problem slides are fine if they help the 5-minute flow.
- Prepare screenshots or a short recording as fallback.
- Keep the testnet deployment stable before the demo.

## Preparation Notes

- Register and log in to MOJO before the competition flow gets busy.
- Register for the event in MOJO. If Luma registration was used, confirm MOJO also shows successful registration.
- After registration or check-in, use the right-side team panel to create a team, invite teammates, accept invitations, or join a team.
- If the right-side team panel is missing, it likely means registration or check-in has not completed; contact Box or the onsite organizer.
- Create or join a team before submission locks team changes. Team changes are still flexible during the event, but no one can join or leave after project submission.
- Only the team lead can submit and edit project details.
- Decide the team lead payout address before submission. Awards are paid through the team lead address, and Monad does not resolve internal team payout disputes.
- Bring laptop, charger, preferred editor, Git, Node.js/npm/yarn, Foundry, and other expected framework tools.
- The venue provides Wi-Fi, power, food, refreshments, mentors, and Monad team support.
